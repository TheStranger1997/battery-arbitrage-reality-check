How much does duration matter?

The same oracle model at 1-, 2- and 4-hour durations (power fixed at 50 MW). Revenue per MW rises with duration, since a 4-hour battery captures more of each spike, but revenue per MWh of capacity falls as the extra storage is dispatched onto shallower parts of the price curve. This is the live GB "2h vs 4h" trade-off.

Key takeaway

  • Achieved wholesale arbitrage is only ~23% of the theoretical maximum. Perfect foresight is impossible, and the biggest price spikes are nearly unforecastable.
  • Even the theoretical maximum (£65,179/MW/yr) sits below all-stream revenue (£72,000/MW/yr). Revenue stacking, not arbitrage, defines the GB BESS investment case.
